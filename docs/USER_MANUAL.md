## Getting Started

### System Requirements

**iOS:**
- iPhone SE (2016) or newer
- iOS 13.0 or later
- 100 MB free storage
- Internet connection (for syncing)

**Android:**
- Android 6.0 (API 23) or later

1. Open **Google Play Store**
2. Search for "Waldo Health"
3. Tap **Install**
4. Open the app
5. Create account or sign in

1. **Create Account**

## Recording an Exposure

### Quick Recording (60 seconds)

1. **Tap the "New" tab** at the bottom of the screen

2. **Select Exposure Type**
   - Silica Dust
   - Asbestos (Class A or B)
   - Hazardous Chemicals
   - Noise
   - Meth Contamination
   - Mould
   - Contaminated Soils
   - Heat Stress
   - Welding Fumes
   - Biological Hazards
   - Radiation

3. **Set Date & Time**
   - Defaults to now
   - Tap to change if documenting a past exposure

4. **Enter Duration**
   - Hours: 0-24
   - Minutes: 0-59
   - Example: "2 hours 30 minutes"

5. **Confirm Location**
   - App automatically detects your GPS location
   - Tap "Use Saved Site" to select from frequently used locations
   - Address is automatically looked up

6. **Take Photos** (up to 5)
   - Tap **Camera** button
   - Take photos of:
     - The exposure area
     - Materials involved
     - PPE being used
     - Any hazard labels
   - Optional: Use **AI Scan** to detect hazards

7. **Select Severity**
   - **Low** - Minor exposure, short duration
   - **Medium** - Moderate exposure, standard work
   - **High** - Significant exposure, requires attention

8. **Record PPE Used**
   - Select all that apply:
     - P2 Respirator
     - Safety Glasses
     - Gloves
     - Ear Protection
     - Hard Hat
     - High-Vis Clothing
     - Safety Boots
     - Face Shield
     - Coveralls

9. **Describe Work Activity**
   - Example: "Cutting concrete slabs"
   - Example: "Demolition of interior walls"
   - Be specific for ACC claims

10. **Add Notes** (optional)
    - Any additional details
    - Control measures taken
    - Weather conditions
    - Equipment used

11. **Tap "Save"**
    - Exposure is saved
    - Syncs automatically when online

## AI Hazard Detection

### What It Does

AI Hazard Detection uses GPT-4 Vision to analyze your photos and:
- Identify visible hazards
- Suggest exposure type
- Recommend appropriate PPE
- Provide safety notes

### How to Use

**Option 1: During New Exposure**
1. Take a photo
2. Tap **AI Scan** button on the photo
3. Wait 5-10 seconds for analysis
4. Review detected hazards
5. Accept or modify suggestions

**Option 2: On Existing Exposure**
1. Open an exposure
2. Tap a photo to enlarge
3. Tap **AI Scan** button
4. Review results

### Understanding Results

**Detected Hazards:**
- List of hazards found in the photo
- Confidence level (percentage)
- Description of what was detected

**Suggested Exposure Type:**
- Most likely type based on photo
- You can accept or change it

**Recommended PPE:**
- Safety equipment you should be wearing
- Based on hazards detected

**Limitations:**
- Requires internet connection
- Can't detect invisible hazards (gases, radiation)
- Professional verification still required for asbestos
- Limited to 50 scans per hour

### Recording a Voice Note

1. While creating an exposure:
2. Tap **Microphone** icon next to Notes field
3. Speak clearly: "Cutting concrete on level 2, very dusty conditions, using water suppression"
4. Tap **Stop** when done
5. Voice is automatically transcribed to text
6. Edit text if needed

### Tips for Voice Entry

- Speak clearly and not too fast
- Mention key details: what, where, how long
- Works offline - transcribes when back online
- Use in noisy environments by getting closer to phone

## Exporting Records

### Why Export?

- ACC claims
- Medical appointments
- Employer requests
- Personal records
- Legal requirements

### Export to PDF

1. Go to **Profile** tab
2. Tap **Export Records**
3. Select **PDF Report**
4. Choose:
   - Date range (Last 30 days, All time, Custom)
   - Exposure types to include
   - Options:
     - ✓ Include Photos
     - ✓ Include Map
     - ✓ Include Full Notes
5. Tap **Generate PDF**
6. Wait while PDF is created
7. Choose where to save or share:
   - Email
   - Files app
   - Cloud storage (Dropbox, Google Drive)
   - Print

### Export to CSV

1. Go to **Profile** tab
2. Tap **Export Records**
3. Select **CSV Spreadsheet**
4. Choose date range and types
5. Tap **Generate CSV**
6. Save or share

**CSV includes:**
- All exposure data in spreadsheet format
- Can open in Excel or Google Sheets
- Good for data analysis

### Export Summary

1. Go to **Profile** tab
2. Tap **Export Records**
3. Select **CSV Summary**
4. Shows statistics:
   - Total exposures
   - Total duration
   - Count by type
   - Count by severity

### Best Practices

- Export monthly for your records
- Keep copies in multiple places
- Export before ACC claim
- Include photos for visual evidence
- Check PDF before sending

## Troubleshooting

### App Won't Open

**Solution:**
1. Restart your phone
2. Check for app updates (App Store / Play Store)
3. Reinstall the app
4. Contact support if still not working

### Can't Take Photos

**Cause:** Camera permission not granted

**Solution:**
1. Go to phone **Settings**
2. Find **Waldo Health**
3. Tap **Permissions**
4. Enable **Camera**
5. Restart app

### GPS Location Not Working

**Cause:** Location permission not granted

**Solution:**
1. Go to phone **Settings**
2. Find **Waldo Health**
3. Tap **Permissions**
4. Enable **Location**
5. Set to **While Using the App**

### Photos Not Uploading

**Cause:** Poor internet connection or large file

**Solution:**
1. Check internet connection
2. Wait for better signal
3. Try uploading later
4. Resize photos if very large

### AI Scan Not Working

**Possible causes:**
- No internet connection
- API rate limit reached (50/hour)
- Server maintenance

**Solution:**
1. Check internet
2. Wait 10 minutes and try again
3. If still not working, contact support

### Can't Export PDF

**Cause:** Not enough storage or permission issue

**Solution:**
1. Free up phone storage
2. Grant file access permission
3. Try exporting fewer records
4. Export as CSV instead

### Exposures Not Syncing

**Cause:** Offline or sync issue

**Solution:**
1. Check internet connection
2. Pull down on Home screen to refresh
3. Go to Profile → Check sync status
4. Tap any failed items to retry

### Forgot Password

1. Tap **Sign In**
2. Tap **Forgot Password?**
3. Enter your email
4. Check email for reset link
5. Create new password

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Waldo Health free?

Yes, Waldo Health is completely free for construction workers.

### Does it work offline?

Yes, you can create exposures offline. They sync when back online.

### How many photos can I add?

Up to 5 photos per exposure.

### Can I use it for ACC claims?

Yes, export your records as PDF for ACC claims.

### Is my data private?

Yes, your data is encrypted and private. We don't share it.

### How long does data stay?

Forever, until you delete your account.

### Can I use it on multiple devices?

Yes, sign in on any device and your data syncs.

### What if I change jobs?

Update your employer in Profile. Your exposure history stays with you.

### Can my employer see my data?

No, only you can see your data unless you export and share it.

### Is AI hazard detection accurate?

It's a helpful tool but not 100% accurate. Always verify with professionals, especially for asbestos.

### Does it drain my battery?

No, Waldo Health is optimized for battery life. GPS only used when creating exposures.

### What about asbestos?

AI can suggest asbestos presence but professional testing is always required before disturbing materials.

### Can I edit past exposures?

Yes, tap any exposure to view and edit details.

### What if I accidentally delete something?

Deleted exposures can't be recovered. Export regularly as backup.

### Is there a web version?

Not yet. Currently iOS and Android apps only.

## Glossary

**ACC** - Accident Compensation Corporation (New Zealand)

**Class A Asbestos** - Friable asbestos (easily crumbled)

**Class B Asbestos** - Non-friable asbestos (bound in material)

**Control Measures** - Actions taken to reduce exposure risk

**Duration** - How long you were exposed

**EXIF** - Photo metadata including GPS and timestamp

**Exposure** - Contact with a hazardous material or condition

**GPS** - Global Positioning System (for location)

**Hazard** - Something that can cause harm

**PPE** - Personal Protective Equipment

**Severity** - How serious the exposure was

**SDS** - Safety Data Sheet (material safety information)

**Silica** - Crystalline silica dust from cutting/grinding concrete, stone, brick

**Sync** - Upload your data to the cloud

**WorkSafe NZ** - New Zealand's workplace health and safety regulator
